# One-Day Meal Plan for Marathon Training (Vegetarian)

Here is a one-day meal plan for a vegetarian athlete in marathon training with carb-loading for endurance performance:

* **Calorie Target:** Approximately 3,200 calories
* **Macronutrient Breakdown:** Protein (110g), Fat (85g), Carbs (420g)

## Meal Plan:

* **Breakfast (7:00 AM):** Oatmeal (100g dry weight) with banana (120g), berries (80g), ground flaxseed (15g), and almond milk (240g).
    * Protein: 12g, Fat: 8g, Carbs: 85g

* **Mid-Morning Snack (10:00 AM):** Whole wheat toast (60g) with peanut butter (25g) and honey (20g).
    * Protein: 10g, Fat: 10g, Carbs: 50g

* **Lunch (1:00 PM):** Chickpea and vegetable curry with chickpeas (150g cooked), mixed vegetables (bell peppers 50g, spinach 50g, carrots 40g), coconut milk (100g), basmati rice (80g cooked).
    * Protein: 15g, Fat: 12g, Carbs: 95g

* **Afternoon Snack (3:30 PM):** Energy bar (homemade or store-bought, 65g) with sports drink (500ml, containing electrolytes).
    * Protein: 8g, Fat: 6g, Carbs: 65g

* **Pre-Dinner Snack (5:30 PM):** Rice cakes (40g) with almond butter (15g) and sliced apple (100g).
    * Protein: 6g, Fat: 9g, Carbs: 45g

* **Dinner (7:00 PM):** Lentil and vegetable pasta with whole wheat pasta (90g dry weight), red lentils (80g cooked), tomato sauce (150g), zucchini (80g), and olive oil (10g). Side salad with mixed greens (100g) and balsamic vinaigrette (15g).
    * Protein: 32g, Fat: 18g, Carbs: 110g

* **Evening Snack (9:00 PM):** Greek yogurt (150g) with granola (40g) and berries (50g).
    * Protein: 17g, Fat: 8g, Carbs: 45g

* **Hydration:** Water throughout the day (minimum 3-4 liters) plus electrolyte drinks during/after training sessions.

This meal plan emphasizes high-carbohydrate intake to maximize glycogen storage, includes adequate plant-based protein from legumes and dairy, and provides sustained energy for marathon training endurance.
